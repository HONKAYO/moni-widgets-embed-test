Will anyone want to be the house?

In FWA, backing is the whole trade. It funds the bid, changes how fast you get picked, and decides how much ETH you keep tied up while you wait. This widget shows where that trade starts to work and where it breaks.

What is fixed

Protocol facts
Inverse weighting by backing. Default 10% acquisition surcharge. Fee split of 94% depositors / 5% crown / 1% protocol. Default standing-bid payout at 85% of backing. Crown threshold at 10% above the largest active deposit.

Model choices
Fair-value anchor, relist friction, reward support, and how crowding translates into fee-sharing positions are assumptions used to make the tradeoff visible.

What the chart shows
Gray line: selection speed relative to a 1 ETH deposit.
Purple line: modeled return on locked ETH under the current activity and crowding assumptions.

The point is not the exact purple number. The point is where the trade breaks: too thin and the position churns before fees compound; too heavy and too much capital sits idle for too little carry. If the purple line jumps at high backing, that is the crown-share threshold turning on, not organic market magic.
